About Us


Who We Are

Students to End Poverty (STEP) is a youth-led organization, founded in late 2019, that mobilizes students to combat poverty through policy advocacy, direct services, and fundraising initiatives.

Mission

Students to End Poverty is a youth-led social justice organization that works to combat poverty in Montgomery County. By mobilizing students through immediate volunteer service projects, lobbying initiatives, fundraising events, and more, we are striving to create positive change in our community.

Learn More About Maryland's Poverty Rates


Statistics

  • More than half a million Marylanders live in poverty, or exactly 576,835.
  • There has been a 19.1 percent increase in individuals living in poverty in Maryland from 1990 to 2016.
  • There has been a 64.3% increase in the poverty rate in Montgomery County from 1990-2016.
  • 12.4 percent of Maryland’s Children live below the poverty line.
  • The poverty rate in Baltimore City is over 21%.

Sources

  • There are over 300,000 children receiving SNAP benefits in Maryland.
  • In Maryland, 13.6% of Black Americans — more than 236,000 people — are living below the poverty line.
  • In Maryland, 640,180 people are facing hunger - and of them 195,960 are children.
  • As of January 2020, Maryland had an estimated 6,360 experiencing homelessness on any given day.
